# Releasing the Shoulders

**Category:** Rest · **Duration:** 4 min · **Moment:** After a long day

> Recording notes: pad ~5s of silence at start and end. Speak slowly.
> `[pause Xs]` markers are silent — don't speak them.
> Save the recording as: `public/audio/releasing-the-shoulders.mp3`

---

[pause 4s]

Sit, or stand, or lie down — wherever you can be still.

[pause 8s]

Bring your attention to the shoulders.

[pause 8s]

Notice where they are right now. Are they up by the ears? Held forward? Tight?

[pause 10s]

You don't have to fix anything. Just notice.

[pause 8s]

Take a slow breath in...

[pause 4s]

...and on the out-breath, let the shoulders drop. As far as they will, without forcing.

[pause 12s]

Again. Breath in...

[pause 4s]

...and out — shoulders down, away from the ears.

[pause 12s]

Now, slowly, roll the shoulders backward. Once. Slowly.

[pause 10s]

And again. Slowly.

[pause 10s]

And one more time. Long and slow.

[pause 10s]

Now release. Let the arms hang.

[pause 12s]

Notice the back of the neck. The space between the shoulder blades.

[pause 12s]

Soften the jaw. The shoulders and jaw are connected. When one tightens, so does the other.

[pause 12s]

One more long breath. Out, even longer than in.

[pause 10s]

Carry this softness with you, for as long as you can.

[pause 6s]
