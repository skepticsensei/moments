# Standing Stillness

**Category:** Practice · **Duration:** 6 min · **Moment:** When you have a few minutes to stand

> Recording notes: pad ~5s of silence at start and end. Speak slowly.
> `[pause Xs]` markers are silent — don't speak them.
> Save the recording as: `public/audio/standing-stillness.mp3`

---

[pause 3s]

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart.

[pause 6s]

Knees soft. Not locked.

[pause 6s]

Pelvis neutral. Tailbone lengthening down toward the floor.

[pause 9s]

Spine long. Shoulders dropping. Head balanced, as if held by a thread from above.

[pause 12s]

Arms hanging at your sides. Or, if you wish, raised in front of you, as if holding a large soft ball.

[pause 14s]

Breathe through the nose. Slow. Quiet. Down into the belly.

[pause 12s]

Notice the body finding small adjustments on its own. Don't fight them.

[pause 12s]

This is not a hold. It is a listening.

[pause 12s]

Stillness is not the absence of movement. It is the presence of attention.

[pause 14s]

Notice the soles of the feet.

[pause 10s]

The weight, the warmth, the contact.

[pause 12s]

Notice the breath, without changing it.

[pause 12s]

Notice the small signals the body is sending. Tightness, tiredness, calm.

[pause 14s]

Let the body settle without collapsing. Soft, but rooted.

[pause 14s]

Like a tree. Held by the ground. Moving with the air.

[pause 14s]

If the mind wanders, return to the feet. The breath. The body.

[pause 14s]

One slow breath in...

[pause 4s]

...and out.

[pause 10s]

Slowly bring the practice to a close. Lower the arms if they are raised.

[pause 10s]

Stand for one more breath.

[pause 6s]

Return softly.

[pause 5s]
