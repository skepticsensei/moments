# Grounding Through Posture

**Category:** Return · **Duration:** 4 min · **Moment:** When you need to come back to yourself

> Recording notes: pad ~5s of silence at start and end. Speak slowly.
> `[pause Xs]` markers are silent — don't speak them.

[pause 4s]

Find a seat. Or stand, if standing feels right.

[pause 8s]

Let your spine lengthen — without forcing.

[pause 10s]

Imagine a thread, gently lifting from the crown of your head.

[pause 12s]

Now soften everything that does not need to be working.

[pause 12s]

The jaw. The brow. The shoulders. The belly.

[pause 15s]

Let the breath move on its own.

[pause 12s]

Notice the weight of the body, settling down through the seat or the feet.

[pause 14s]

The earth is supporting you.

[pause 12s]

You are not held up by effort. You are held by the ground.

[pause 15s]

One slow breath in...

[pause 4s]

...and out.

[pause 12s]

This is what center feels like. Not stiff. Not collapsed. Steady.

[pause 14s]

Carry a little of this with you when you stand and move on.

[pause 10s]

One more breath.

[pause 4s]

When you are ready, return.
